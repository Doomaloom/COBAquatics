1. Demonstrate At Least 2 Different Entries with Different Aids
   • Eyes up and forward on entry
   • Control of aid on entry

2. Entry With Aid and Swim 25 m or yd Head-up Front Crawl or Breaststroke. Assume Ready Position and Demonstrate Ability to Scull Forward, Backward and Turn
   • Control of aid on entry
   • Eyes above the surface during swim
   • Basic arm and leg mechanics
   • Distance completed non-stop
   • Ready position with effective support scull showing ability to move forward, backward and turn

3. Demonstrate Defence Methods from the Front, Side and Rear
   • Avoidance of grasp(s)
   • Ready position at a safe distance
   • Constructive communication
   • Treading water throughout

4. Demonstrate Eggbeater Kick Showing Ability to Travel, Change Direction and Height Levels
   • Vertical body position
   • Movement pattern of kick resembles whip kick with alternate leg action
   • Drive with knees apart; feet wider than knees
   • Head above surface throughout; change in height on command
   • Travel 1-2 m or yd in any direction

5.Carry a 4.5 kg (10 lb) Object 25 m or yd Using Swimmer's Choice of Lifesaving Kick
   • Basic lifesaving kick mechanics with control of direction
   • Distance completed non-stop
   • Head remains above the surface

6. Demonstrate the Removal of an Unconscious Victim with the Assistance of an Untrained Bystander
   • Clear and appropriate direction for bystander
   • Appropriate choice of removal
   • Safe removal without injury to rescuer, bystander or victim

7. Head-up Approach into Head-first Surface Dive to a Maximum Depth of 2 m Swim Underwater for 5-10 m or yd and Surface.  Foot-first Surface Dive (max depth 2m) Recover an Object and Return It to the Starting Point
   • Eyes up and forward on approach
   • Vertical descent
   • Travel in designated direction
   • Eyes open underwater
   • Object recovered and returned
   • Arm extended above head on ascent

 

8. Demonstrate in Shallow Water, the Ability to Turn a Victim Face-up and Support the Face Above the Surface
   • Victim's face supported above surface
   • Airway maintained throughout; water prevented from entering victim's nose or mouth
   • Victim quickly secured at nearest point of safety

9. Swim Each of the Following Strokes 100 m or yd: Front Crawl, Back Crawl, Breaststroke
   • Minimum Distance completed (for each stroke)
   • Front Crawl
     - Alternate arm action with overarm recovery and underwater pull
     - Hand entry beyond shoulder, pull past hip
     - Alternate leg action drive
     - Rhythmic kick
     - Regular breathing pattern to side with underwater exhalation
     - Continuous propulsion
   • Back Crawl
     - Alternate arm action with overarm recovery and underwater pull
     - Hand entry beyond shoulder, pull past hip
     - Alternate leg action drive
     - Rhythmic kick
     - Continuous propulsion
   • Breaststroke
     - Symmetrical arm recovery and drive
     - Regular breathing pattern with underwater exhalation
     - Both legs kick at same time and recover at the same time
     - Drive with knees apart; feet wider than knees
     - Continuous propulsion

10. Complete a 600 m or yd Workout At Least 3 Times During the Training Course.
   • Warm-up:
     - 25 m or yd Lifesaving kick,
     - 50 m or yd Back Crawl,
     - 50 m or yd Breaststroke,
     - 50 m or yd Front Crawl
   • Stroke drills:
     - 5 x 25 m or yd Instructor's choice
   • Work Set:
     - 4 x 50 m or yd on 90 sec Instructor's choice
   • Cool down:
     - 100 m or yd Swimmer's choice
   • Workout is completed at least 3 times during the training course
   • Ability to locate pulse and count beats per minute
   • Ability to adapt pace in accordance with desired heart rate target

 

11. Swim 300 m in 9 min. or Better (300 yd in 8:00 min) Using Any Stroke
or Combination of Strokes of the Swimmer's Choice
   • Recognizable stroke(s) used
   • Distance completed with a uniform pace
   • Time limit met or bettered

12. Demonstrate the Recognition and Care of a Bone or Joint Injury
   • Assessment of environment for hazards
   • Establish responsiveness
   • Recognition of injury based on pain, obvious deformity or swelling and the
   probable cause
   • Movement of injury minimized to prevent further injury
   • If available, ice applied to injury site
   • EMS activated of necessary

13. Demonstrate the Recognition and Care of a Victim Suffering Respiratory
Distress from Asthma or Severe Allergic Reaction
   • Assessment of environment for hazards
   • Establish responsiveness
   • Determination of cause (and removal if possible) of emergency
   • Victim placed in most comfortable position
   • Victim assisted to find and use medication (asthma inhaler or epinephrine
   auto-injector)
   • Activate EMS if necessary

14. From 3 Different Heights or Positions, Locate and Describe an Object
Located on the Bottom or Below the Surface
   • Ability to identify the barriers to recognition
   • Move to a position to enable candidate to best "see" the object

15. Perform a Rescue of a Weak or Non-Swimmer with a Towing Aid. Swim
Approach 20 m or yd and Tow Victim to Safety Showing Ability to Avoid
Contact. Rescuer Performs Appropriate Follow Up Procedures, Including
Treatment for Shock
   • Quick accurate recognition
   • Personal safety maintained
   • Appropriate assessment of situation
   • Call for help
   • Appropriate choice and use of aid
   • Appropriate entry and efficient approach
   • Appropriate choice of tow for the circumstance
   • Victim secured at nearest point of safety
   • Appropriate care of victim and follow-up
   • Visual contact and constructive communication throughout
   • Appropriate use of bystanders when available
   • Victim observed/reassured until rescuer relieved of responsibility
